Flip the ratio.

For as long as digital products have existed, one designer has worked with five, ten, twenty engineers. Nobody questioned it — building software was the slow, expensive part, so that’s where the people went.

AI flipped the economics. Code that used to take a quarter now takes days, and every product organization is saying the same thing: design has become the bottleneck, because development is so much faster now. The constraint moved. The ratio didn’t.

So here’s a simple solution: flip it. Twenty designers for every engineer. Problem solved. You’re welcome. Let me know how the budget approval goes.

Except it has stopped being a joke. At 2FRESH, we are now working on a project where the design budget is double the engineering budget — a first in our history, and something unheard of in the digital product industry. When making things is cheap, deciding what to make — and making it good — is where the value goes.
